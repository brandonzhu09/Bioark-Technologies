Gene Knock-Out

The product provides a rapid and efficient approach to disrupt gene expression for both research and therapeutic applications.

Technical Background

CRISPR knockout (KO) is a revolutionary gene-editing technique that allows researchers to disrupt or "knock out" specific genes within an organism's genome. The CRISPR-Cas9 system is the most widely used tool for this purpose, harnessing a natural bacterial defense mechanism against viral infections.

Key Components

  1. Cas9 Protein: An endonuclease that introduces double-strand breaks (DSBs) in the DNA at specific genomic locations.
  2. Guide RNA (gRNA): A short RNA sequence that directs the Cas9 protein to the target gene by complementary base pairing.
  3. Repair Pathways: Once the DSB is created, the cell's repair machinery activates. The primary pathways for repair are:
    • Non-Homologous End Joining (NHEJ): This repair mechanism often results in insertions or deletions (indels) at the break site, leading to frameshift mutations that can disrupt gene function.
    • Homology-Directed Repair (HDR): Less common for knockout purposes, HDR can be utilized when a donor template is provided, but is typically used for precise edits rather than knockouts.

By utilizing the NHEJ repair pathway, researchers can effectively create gene knockouts, leading to a loss of gene function.

Experimental Process

  1. Design of gRNA:
    • The gRNA is designed to target a specific gene of interest, typically located in a conserved region of the gene to maximize the likelihood of knockout.
    • Various online tools can be used to design gRNA sequences with high specificity and minimal off-target effects.
  2. Delivery of Components:
    • The Cas9 protein and gRNA can be delivered into target cells using our kits, utilizing the corresponding transfection methods.
    • Plasmid Transfection: Introducing plasmid DNA encoding Cas9 and gRNA into cells.
    • Viral Vectors: Using lentiviruses or AAVs to deliver the editing components into hard-to-transfect cells.
  3. Screening and Validation:
    • PCR Screening: Amplifying the target region using specific primers to check for indel formation.
    • Functional Assays: Analyzing the impact of the knockout on cellular behavior, such as proliferation, apoptosis, or response to treatments.
    • Successful knockouts are validated through techniques such as Western blotting or immunofluorescence to confirm the absence of the target protein.
    • Further functional studies may be performed to assess the biological impact of the gene knockout.
