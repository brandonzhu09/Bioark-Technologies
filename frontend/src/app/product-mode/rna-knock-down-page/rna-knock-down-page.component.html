CRISPR RNA Knock-down

The product offers a more specific and potent alternative to traditional RNAi methods for knocking down RNA expression levels.

Technical Background

CRISPR RNA Knockdown utilizing Cas13 is an innovative approach to post-transcriptional gene regulation. Unlike the Cas9 protein used in traditional CRISPR gene editing for introducing double-strand breaks, Cas13 is an RNA-guided RNA endonuclease that specifically targets and cleaves RNA molecules. This ability makes it an effective tool for knocking down gene expression by degrading messenger RNA (mRNA) transcripts.

Key Components:

  • Cas13 Protein: A member of the CRISPR family that targets RNA instead of DNA. Cas13 is derived from certain bacterial species, such as Leptotrichia shahii and Pseudomonas aeruginosa.
  • Guide RNA (gRNA): A synthetic RNA molecule designed to bind to the target mRNA. It contains a sequence complementary to the target RNA, directing Cas13 to the specific mRNA for cleavage.

Experimental Process

Designing gRNA

  • gRNA is designed to specifically bind to the mRNA of the gene of interest. Tools are available to assist in selecting optimal gRNA sequences that minimize off-target effects.

Delivery of CRISPR Components

  • Plasmid Transfection: Introducing plasmid DNA encoding Cas13 and gRNA into cells.
  • Viral Vectors: Using lentiviruses or AAVs to deliver the components into hard-to-transfect cells.

Validation

  • Quantitative PCR (qPCR): Measuring the levels of the target mRNA to confirm successful knockdown.
  • Western Blotting: Assessing the corresponding protein levels to validate the reduction in gene expression.
