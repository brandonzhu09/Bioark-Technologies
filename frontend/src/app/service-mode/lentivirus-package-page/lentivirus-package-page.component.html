Lentivirus Package Services

Lentivirus packaging services provide researchers with the tools and components needed to produce lentiviral vectors for gene delivery into a variety of target cells, including dividing and non-dividing cells. Lentiviral vectors are derived from the Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) and are widely used in gene therapy, functional genomics, and the development of stable cell lines.

Technology Background

Lentivirus Vectors Construction

The construction of lentiviral vectors commonly utilizes a three-plasmid system, which includes:

  • Transfer Plasmid: Contains the gene of interest and a promoter to drive expression.
  • Packaging Plasmid: Provides the necessary viral proteins (gag, pol, and rev) required for virus assembly.
  • Envelope Plasmid: Provides the envelope glycoprotein (e.g., VSV-G) that allows the virus to enter target cells.

Production Process

  • Transfection: The three plasmids are co-transfected into suitable producer cells (e.g., HEK293T cells) using a transfection reagent.
  • Virus Assembly and Release: The cells produce lentiviral particles, which are released into the culture medium.
  • Collection and Concentration: The lentiviral supernatant is harvested, filtered, and concentrated (if necessary) to yield a high-titer viral stock.
