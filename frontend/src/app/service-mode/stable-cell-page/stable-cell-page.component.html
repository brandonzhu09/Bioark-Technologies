Technology Background

Stable Cell Line Development

Stable cell lines are crucial for consistent and reproducible studies in gene function, drug discovery, and therapeutic research. The establishment of a stable cell line involves integrating a gene of interest into the host cell genome, allowing for continuous expression over many passages.

Vector Design

The choice of vector and the method of integration (random vs. targeted) play significant roles in the functionality and stability of the expressed protein. Our service focuses on minimizing random integration events, which can lead to silencing of the transgene or disruption of endogenous genes.

Transfection Methods

Different cell types necessitate various transfection techniques to achieve optimal expression. Currently, we offer two methods:

  • Standard Vectors: This approach offers lower integration rates and is ideal for delivering larger transgenes without disrupting host genes.
  • Lentiviral Transduction: This method allows for stable integration into the host genome, enabling the long-term expression of the gene of interest, even in non-dividing cells.

Selection and Validation

Employing selective markers ensures that only cells containing the desired genetic modifications are expanded. Subsequent validation techniques confirm successful integration and expression, providing assurance of experimental reliability.

Service Content

Customizable Cell Line Development

We offer tailored services to develop stable cell lines according to specific project requirements, including different cell types and functional characteristics.

Vector Construction

Design and construction of plasmid vectors containing the desired gene or genetic modification, often including selectable markers for efficient screening.

Transfection Techniques

  • Lipofection: For adherent cells.
  • Electroporation: For hard-to-transfect cell types.
  • Viral Transduction: Using lentiviruses or AAVs to deliver genetic material into target cells.

Selection and Expansion

  • Application of selective pressure (e.g., antibiotic selection) to isolate successfully transfected cells.
  • Expansion of selected clones in controlled environments.

Validation of Stable Cell Lines

  • Genomic Analysis: Verification of gene integration via PCR or sequencing.
  • Expression Analysis: Assessing the expression levels of the target gene using techniques such as qPCR, Western blotting, or flow cytometry.

Cryopreservation

Preservation of stable cell lines in liquid nitrogen for long-term storage and future use.

Technical Support

Ongoing consultation and support to help clients optimize their experiments using the generated cell lines.

Applications

The generated stable cell lines can be used for a wide range of applications, including drug screening, functional genomics, protein production, and mechanistic studies of disease.
